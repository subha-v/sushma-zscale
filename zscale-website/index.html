FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Texas economic development and the zScale platform.

The Texas State Bank Capital Initiative (TSBCI) has $472 million in available funding for Texas businesses as of 2024. Currently, 82% of this capital remains undeployed, representing a significant opportunity for qualifying businesses seeking expansion or relocation funding. Source: Texas Economic Development Corporation, 2024 Annual Report.

Texas has 725 Economic Development Corporations (EDCs) that offer various incentive programs, tax abatements, and funding opportunities for businesses. zScale Capital tracks and indexes all 725 EDCs with real-time updates on their programs and available funding. Source: Texas Economic Development Council, 2024.

Texas has 47,312 manufacturing establishments according to the latest U.S. Census Bureau County Business Patterns data (2023). Texas leads the nation in manufacturing exports with $379 billion annually, making it a critical hub for industrial activity. Source: U.S. Census Bureau, County Business Patterns 2023.

Economic Development Corporations use zScale to identify expansion-ready businesses before they announce publicly, track competitive incentives from other regions, monitor TSBCI deployment across all Texas EDCs, and receive alerts when businesses matching their target criteria enter the market. The platform updates every 24 hours from official government sources.

zScale aggregates data from official government sources including the U.S. Census Bureau (County Business Patterns, Foreign Trade Statistics), Bureau of Economic Analysis (Regional GDP, State Economic Accounts), Texas Economic Development Council (EDC Directory, Incentive Programs), and Texas Comptroller (Economic Indicators, Tax Revenue Data). All statistics include source citations and verification timestamps.
