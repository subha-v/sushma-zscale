Transparent Data

Our Methodology

How we collect, verify, and maintain Texas economic development data from official government sources.

TL;DR: zScale aggregates data from 4 primary official sources: U.S. Census Bureau (business patterns, trade data), Bureau of Economic Analysis (GDP, economic accounts), Texas Economic Development Council (EDC directory, incentives), and Texas Comptroller (tax data, economic indicators). All data is refreshed every 24 hours with full source citations.

Data Integrity

Verifiable, Cited, Transparent

Every statistic on zScale includes a source citation and verification timestamp. We believe in radical transparency.

Primary Sources

Official Government Data Sources

U.S. Census Bureau

County Business Patterns provides establishment counts (47,312 Texas manufacturers). Foreign Trade Statistics tracks exports ($379B annually). Updated annually with quarterly supplements.

census.gov

Bureau of Economic Analysis

Regional GDP data (4.7% Texas growth) and State Economic Accounts. Quarterly updates with annual revisions. Official source for economic growth metrics.

bea.gov

Texas Economic Development Council

Complete directory of 725 Texas EDCs with incentive program details. TSBCI funding tracking ($472M available, 82% undeployed). Updated monthly.

texasedc.org

Texas Comptroller

Tax revenue data, economic indicators, and fiscal analysis. Provides state-level economic health metrics and business activity tracking.

comptroller.texas.gov

Our Process

Data Collection & Verification

1. Automated Collection

Our system automatically pulls data from official APIs and public datasets every 24 hours. This includes Census Bureau APIs, BEA data releases, and Texas state government data portals.

2. Cross-Verification

Data points are cross-referenced across multiple sources when available. For example, manufacturer counts are verified against both Census Bureau and Texas Workforce Commission data.

3. Source Attribution

Every statistic displayed includes the original source, publication date, and last verification timestamp. This ensures users can verify any claim independently.

4. Regular Audits

Monthly audits compare our displayed data against source documents. Any discrepancies are investigated and corrected within 24 hours of discovery.
